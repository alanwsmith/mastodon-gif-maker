Mastodon GIF Maker

Welcome In

Hi there! I'm Alan and I'm glad you stopped by.

I was disappointed to learn Twitter is prohibiting links to other social networks. I want to let my followers know I've moved to Mastodon, but I don't want to run afoul of their rules. So, I setup this GIF maker as a means of communication. You're welcome to use it too.

NOTE: It sounds like maybe Twitter isn't prohibiting links anymore? If that's the case, I'm glad the necessity for this goes away. I'm still gonna play with it though because I'm having a blast. And, maybe the goal becomes to make and post as many as possible :-)

Warning

I wouldn't be surpised if the rules of engagement change again to prohibit GIFs. I'm using this this with the full knowledge that it may be the last thing I do on Twitter. I'm fine with that. I want to make sure the people I've connected on the platform know where I've gone before my ability to communicate with them is taken away.

Said another way: Use at your own risk

Make Your GIF

With all that in mind, here's the process for making an "I've move to Mastodon" GIF

1. Pick A Background GIF

NOTE: I'm adding more GIFs, if you have art that's open source or that you own and are willing to share, I'd love to see about inclding it. Hit me up.

2. Update Your Text

Change this as much as you'd like and you can preview it a little further below.






3. Check Your Layout

This preview doesn't animate yet. You can get an idea from the thumbnails further above. Or, hit "Create It" as many times as you'd like.

NOTE: The text for line 5 is shifted to the right to accomodate an overlay icon that shows up in the Twitter web page interface.

4. And Create Your GIF!

(NOTE 1: It can take a few seconds to generate - NOTE 2: The image is generated in a pop-up window which your browser will probably block the first time. Give it permission, try again and it should work. )

Save the image from the pop-up window and then you can post it on twitter. (NOTE 3: You might need to add the filename including `.gif` at the end.)

5. Post It

That's it for making the GIFs. After you download it, you can post it on Twitter at your leisure. The rest of the page is notes about this project. If you're interested in more details, read on.
